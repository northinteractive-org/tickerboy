How this is calculated

The gauge is your chance per single approach — the product of independent factors: single → open to dating → accepts your age → clears the height bar → background → venue receptive (adjusted for timing) → your delivery, then nudged by your logged outcomes.

The "≥1 this outing" number is cumulative: it compounds your per-approach odds across all the women in your age range at the venue, so widening your range or picking a busier spot raises it — even though your per-approach average can dip. Background uses small, population-level multipliers from aggregate dating-app reply-rate data (controversial, not about any individual); pick "Prefer not to say" to keep it neutral.

Numbers are directional estimates from public research (U.S. Census/ACS marital status, Pew Research on singles, OkCupid age-preference data, height-preference surveys). With a free Census API key added, the "single" factor is refined with real local data for your county. This is entertainment and a nudge toward what you control — not a guarantee. Data & references

  • U.S. Census Bureau — American Community Survey (ACS): marital status by age and sex; local county data via the ACS 5-year API.
  • Pew Research Center: share of single adults open to dating or a relationship.
  • Christian Rudder, Dataclysm / OkCupid data: age-preference patterns in dating.
  • Height-preference research, e.g. Stulp et al. (2013): women's partner-height preferences.
  • FCC Area API: latitude/longitude → county (keyless reverse geocoding).

Venue receptivity, foot-traffic, timing, and delivery figures are the author's estimates, not measured rates. Curves are fit by judgment, not regression. This tool is for entertainment and self-reflection — approach people respectfully and read the room.
