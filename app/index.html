How this is calculated

The gauge is your chance on one approach. We multiply the factors that all have to line up: she's single, she wants to date, she's open to your age, your height, your look, your background, how open the venue is, and your delivery. Then we tune it to the outcomes you log.

The "leave with a number" figure is cumulative. It stacks your per-approach odds across every woman your age in the room, so a wider range or a busier spot lifts it even when your per-approach average dips. Background uses tiny population-level numbers from aggregate dating-app data. It says nothing about you, and Prefer not to say keeps it neutral.

These are directional estimates from public research: U.S. Census and ACS marital status, Pew Research on singles, OkCupid age-preference data, and height-preference studies. Add a free Census API key and the "single" number uses real data for your county. Treat it as a guide, not a guarantee. Always approach with respect and read the room.

Data & references

  • U.S. Census Bureau, American Community Survey: marital status by age and sex, plus local county data via the ACS 5-year API.
  • Pew Research Center: share of single adults open to dating or a relationship.
  • Christian Rudder, Dataclysm / OkCupid data: age-preference patterns in dating.
  • Height-preference research, e.g. Stulp et al. (2013): women's partner-height preferences.
  • FCC Area API: turns your location into a county (no key needed).

Venue, foot-traffic, timing, and delivery numbers are our estimates, not measured rates. Treat the whole thing as a guide for building a real-world skill, not gospel. Approach with respect and read the room.
